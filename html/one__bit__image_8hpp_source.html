38 /*
39  Copy (or transform) bits from a one-bit source bitmap to a one-bit destination bitmap.
40 
41  The source bitmap's first byte is at src; src_scan_line_stride describes the distance (in bytes)
42  between (x, y) and (x, y+1). src_size describes the width and height (in bits) of the source's
43  readable memory.
44 
45  dst, dst_scan_line_stride and dst_size describe the same properties of the destination bitmap.
46 
47  src_rect_to_copy identifies a subrectangle in source space that is copied/transformed to the
48  rectangle in destination space with the same size, whose top left is given by dst_location.
49  If either of these rectangles spill outside of the readable/writable areas of their corresponding
50  bitmaps, they are both truncated such that the mapping is preserved but only for points inside
51  their respective bitmaps.
52 
53  src_size, dst_size, src_rect_to_copy and dst_location all describe quantities of _bits_, not
54  bytes.
55 
56  "op" is a member of the bit_op enumeration above and defines the transfer operation applied to
57  the bits in the source and destination.
58 */
